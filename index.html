Strumming Pattern Guide

▼

Straight Down

D  D  D  D
Simple quarter note downstrokes. Good for punk and driving rock.
Songs: Seven Nation Army, Fell in Love with a Girl, Start Me Up

Basic Folk/Country

D  D U  U D U
The most common beginner pattern. Works for tons of acoustic songs.
Songs: Knockin' on Heaven's Door, Free Fallin', Heart of Gold, Blowin' in the Wind, Wild World, Father and Son

Island/Reggae Strum

D  D U . U D U
Similar to basic folk but with a skip that gives it a bouncy feel.
Songs: I Shot the Sheriff, Redemption Song, Hotel Yorba

Slow Ballad

D . . U . U D U
Spacious pattern for slow songs, lets chords breathe.
Songs: Wish You Were Here, Landslide, Going to California, The Rain Song

Driving Rock

D D U D D U
Harder, more rhythmic feel for rock songs.
Songs: Brown Sugar, Gimme Shelter, Wild Horses, Won't Back Down

Blues Shuffle

D.U D.U D.U D.U
Shuffle/swing feel with a triplet groove. Standard 12-bar blues feel.
Songs: The Thrill Is Gone, Sweet Home Chicago, Hoochie Coochie Man, Cross Road Blues

16th Note Funk

D x U x D x U D
Tight, percussive strumming for funk and R&B.
Songs: Use Me, Let's Get It On, Ain't No Sunshine

Fingerpicking Arpeggio

T 1 2 3 2 1
Not a strum but a picking pattern (T=thumb, 1-3=fingers). For fingerpicked songs.
Songs: Dust in the Wind, Scarborough Fair, The Boxer, Landslide, Babe I'm Gonna Leave You
